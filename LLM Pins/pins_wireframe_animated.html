How do I use the dative case with movement verbs?
Claude

In German, movement verbs like gehen and fahren take the accusative when showing direction, but the dative when showing location.

The key rule: accusative answers "where to?" (wohin), dative answers "where?" (wo). pin

So: Ich gehe in den Park (accusative) vs Ich bin im Park (dative).

You
What about two-way prepositions?
Claude

Two-way prepositions like in, an, auf work with both cases depending on context.

Movement = accusative. Static location = dative. Think of it as direction vs position.
